# Securing the Future: Enterprise-Grade Trust in a Web3 World

The traditional web has spent 30 years building identity and auth. Web3 throws most of that out — and rightly so. But the new model has to be **at least as secure** as the old one. Here's how EPSX approaches it.

## The principles

1. **Wallet-first** — Your wallet is your identity. No email, no password, no KYC for basic features.
2. **No custodial risk** — We never hold your private keys. We never can.
3. **Signed actions, not sessions** — Critical actions (withdrawals, settings changes) require a fresh wallet signature, not just a session cookie.
4. **Transparent permissions** — Every role and scope is visible to you, in plaintext, before you sign.
5. **Replay protection** — Every signature has a unique nonce, a 5-minute expiry, and a one-time use.

## How SIWE works in EPSX

**Sign-In With Ethereum (EIP-4361)** is the standard. The flow:

1. User clicks "Connect Wallet" in our app
2. App requests a **challenge** from our identity service (`POST /api/v1/identity/auth/challenge` with `{address, chain_id}`)
3. Identity service generates a random 17-char nonce, stores it in Redis with a 5-minute TTL, and returns a fully-formed SIWE message
4. App asks the wallet to `personal_sign` the message
5. App posts the signature back (`POST /api/v1/identity/auth/siwe` with `{message, signature, chain_id}`)
6. Identity service:
   - Looks up the nonce in Redis (and **deletes it** to prevent replay)
   - Verifies the signature against the message
   - Issues a real JWT (signed with HS256)
   - Returns the JWT + a refresh token
7. App stores the JWT in an `HttpOnly`, `SameSite=Lax` cookie — never in `localStorage`
8. Subsequent API calls send the JWT as `Authorization: Bearer <token>` or in the cookie

## What we don't do

- **No `localStorage` tokens** — XSS-safe by design
- **No refresh tokens in cookies** — they're returned to the client which decides where to store them
- **No long-lived sessions** — access tokens expire in 1 hour, refresh tokens in 7 days
- **No "remember me" for sensitive actions** — withdrawals always require a fresh signature

## Threat model

We assume:

- The user's browser is reasonably up-to-date
- The user's wallet is honest (hardware wallets recommended)
- The DNS is honest (we use DNSSEC where possible)
- Our infrastructure is partially compromised (zero-trust between services)

We do NOT assume:

- The user's email is secure (we don't ask for one)
- The user's phone is secure (no SMS 2FA)
- The network is private (everything is TLS 1.3)

## What we're working on

- **Session-bound cookies** — Bind the session cookie to the wallet address so a stolen cookie is useless
- **Hardware wallet attestation** — Verify that the signing device is what it claims to be
- **Multi-sig for high-value accounts** — Require N-of-M signatures for merchant accounts above a threshold
- **Bug bounty** — Coming Q3 2026
