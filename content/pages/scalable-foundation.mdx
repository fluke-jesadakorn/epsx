# Built for Ambition: A Scalable Foundation for Global Analytics

When we set out to build EPSX, the brief was deceptively simple: a platform that delivers **institutional-grade market intelligence** to anyone with a wallet. The hard part wasn't the analytics — it was the foundation underneath.

## Microservices from day one

Every subsystem in EPSX is a standalone Rust service with a single responsibility:

- **Identity** — SIWE authentication, JWT issuance, role-based access control
- **Wallet** — On-chain read paths, ERC-20 balance lookups, transaction history
- **Payment** — Paymaster coordination, gas sponsorship, USDC settlement
- **Subscription** — Per-merchant vaults, billing schedules, grace periods
- **Content** — Visual page builder, MDX rendering, asset registry
- **Notification** — Real-time alerts, multi-channel delivery
- **Analytics** — Event ingestion, Prometheus metrics, business dashboards
- **Indexer** — Multi-chain block scanning, transaction decoding

A single **gateway** sits in front of them, rewriting paths and handling cross-cutting concerns. This means any service can scale independently, fail independently, and be replaced without touching the others.

## Why Rust?

Three reasons:

1. **Performance** — Sub-millisecond p99 latency on hot paths, even under load
2. **Safety** — The borrow checker catches entire classes of bugs at compile time
3. **Type system** — Domain models are encoded in the type signatures, not just docs

We use **axum** for HTTP, **sqlx** for Postgres, **redis** for caching and ephemeral state, and **alloy** for on-chain interactions. Every binary is statically linked and runs in our local Kubernetes cluster.

## What scales with you

- **Block registry** is dynamic — add a new chain by dropping a config entry
- **Per-merchant vaults** mean each business has isolated billing state
- **CDN-friendly frontends** with server-side rendered shells and lazy-loaded islands
- **Postgres + Redis + MinIO** on bare metal, no managed-service lock-in
