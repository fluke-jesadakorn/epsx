# Smarter Decisions: How EPSX AI Navigates Market Complexity

The crypto market doesn't sleep, and neither does the signal-to-noise problem. Every minute, thousands of tokens move, hundreds of contracts deploy, and dozens of narratives shift. **EPSX AI** is our answer: a single, explainable model that synthesizes everything into a ranking you can act on.

## What EPSX AI does

The model takes a token (or wallet, or contract) and produces:

- A **0–100 momentum score** updated every 15 minutes
- A **narrative fit** rating against trending sectors (L2s, AI, RWA, DeFi, memes)
- A **risk surface** map highlighting concentration, liquidity, and holder distribution
- A **confidence interval** so you know when to trust the score and when to dig deeper

The output is a card in the [Rankings](/rankings) page, with the score, the inputs, and a one-click "explain" button that walks through the math.

## How the model works

Three layers feed into the final score:

### 1. On-chain fundamentals

- Holder count and growth rate
- Top-10 concentration (lower is healthier)
- Liquidity depth on the major DEXs
- Transfer volume vs. swap volume (a high ratio can mean airdrop farming)
- Contract age and audit status

### 2. Off-chain signals

- Social mention velocity (X, Farcaster, Lens)
- Developer activity (commits, deployments, issue close rates)
- News coverage tone (via our news + sentiment indexer)

### 3. Quantitative

- Realized volatility vs. implied
- Funding rate skew (perp markets)
- Open interest delta
- Order-book imbalance

Each layer is a separate model with its own confidence weight. The final score is a **weighted geometric mean** of the three, so a single bad input can't tank a token — but consistent weakness across all three will.

## Why explainable?

Most AI trading tools give you a black-box score. We think that's dangerous. Every EPSX AI score comes with a breakdown showing:

- Which inputs contributed most (positive and negative)
- Which inputs were noisy or missing
- What would push the score up or down

If a token's score is 72, you should be able to look at the breakdown and say, "Ah, it's high because of social momentum but low on-chain fundamentals — I'll wait for the fundamentals to catch up."
